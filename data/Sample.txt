This project presents a state-of-the-art AI Research Assistant leveraging Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and Multi-Agent Orchestration. The system addresses the "context window" and "hallucination" limitations of standard Large Language Models (LLMs) by implementing a Hybrid Search Pipeline (Vector + Keyword) and a Self-Correction Loop. By integrating GraphRAG for multi-hop reasoning, the assistant can synthesize information across disjointed research papers with high factual precision. Experimental results using the Ragas framework demonstrate a significant improvement in Faithfulness and Answer Relevancy compared to baseline RAG architectures.
